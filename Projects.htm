P.A.C.R.R. 2.0

P.A.C.R.R. 2.0 is an autonomous quadrupedal platform designed to enable accurate mapping and characterization of environments which may be too hazardous or constrained for personnel. To localize itself, P.A.C.R.R uses an Inertial Measurement Unit to measure its linear acceleration and angular momentum, which is then combined with information from two RGB cameras to enable mapping of the local 3D environment (pictured in slides 2 and 3). This information is then processed using an onboard NVIDIA Jetson, which can broadcast the map to users or perform path planning using modeled 3d space.

Flashlight Printed Circuit Board

This circuit was designed in AutoCAD to safely charge an attached lithium ion battery, and then use the charged battery to power a bank of LEDs with variable input voltage facilitated by a dimming potentiometer. I added on a laser cut wooden case in order to protect the circuit and attached battery.
